What Is a Fakemon?

A Fakemon is a made-up battle creature that you design! It lives inside a JSON file — a special text file the game reads when it starts. You decide its name, what it looks like, how strong it is, and what moves it can use.

💡 The game figures out your Fakemon's power level on its own by running 200 pretend battles in the background. So the game will always find you a fair opponent — no matter how strong your Fakemon is!
2

The 5 Stats

Every Fakemon has five stats. These numbers describe how good your Fakemon is at different things. Some moves can raise or lower these stats mid-battle!

❤️ Health
field: "hp"
How much damage your Fakemon can take before fainting. When this hits zero, you lose!
Suggested: 80–140
⚔️ Power
field: "power"
A multiplier on all your damage numbers. Higher power = every attack hits harder.
Suggested: 80–120
💨 Speed
field: "speed"
When two moves have the same speed tier (like both are "medium"), the Fakemon with higher speed goes first.
Suggested: 60–120
🛡️ Defense
field: "defense"
Reduces incoming damage by a percentage. A defense of 20 means you only take 80% of the damage you'd normally receive.
Suggested: 0–40
🎯 Accuracy
field: "accuracy"
The base chance (%) that your moves will actually hit the target. Status effects like confuse or paralyze can reduce this!
Suggested: 70–100
💡 StatUp and StatDwn moves raise or lower a specific stat temporarily during battle. You pick which stat gets boosted using statTarget — see Section 5 for details!
3

Types & Matchups

Every Fakemon has a type. Types work like a big game of rock-paper-scissors — some types hit super hard against certain types, and not very hard against others.

Here are all the types:

🔥 Fire 💧 Water 🌬️ Air 🌍 Earth ⚡ Electric ❄️ Ice 🟣 Poison 🌑 Dark 👻 Ghost ⚙️ Metal 🌿 Nature ⭐ Normal
ℹ️ Type matchups give ×2 damage when super effective or ×0.5 when not very effective. Check the guide for the full matchup chart!

Status Effects

Moves can apply status effects — special conditions that stick around for multiple turns and mess with a fighter's stats or actions. There are two categories:

🔴 Primary Statuses — Only one at a time!

A Fakemon can only have one of these at once. If it already has burn, you can't also poison it.

🔴
Burn
PRIMARY
Deals 6% max HP damage every turn. Also lowers ATK by 20 while active.
⏱ Lasts until cured
🟣
Poison
PRIMARY
Deals 8% max HP damage every turn — the deadliest damage-over-time status.
⏱ Lasts until cured
⚡
Paralyze
PRIMARY
35% chance to skip your turn each turn. Also lowers Speed by 30 while active.
⏱ Lasts until cured
💤
Sleep
PRIMARY
Skips 2–3 turns completely. No moves, no actions. Then wakes up automatically.
⏱ 2–3 turns then wakes

🔵 Secondary Statuses — Can stack with primary ones!

😵
Confuse
SECONDARY
33% chance to hurt itself (8–15 damage) instead of acting each turn.
⏱ 2–4 turns
😦
Flinch
SECONDARY
Skips one action immediately. Only works if you attacked first this turn!
⏱ 1 turn only
📈
Stat Up
SECONDARY
Temporarily boosts one of YOUR OWN stats. You choose which stat with statTarget. See table below!
⏱ 3 turns then fades
📉
Stat Down
SECONDARY
Temporarily lowers one of the ENEMY's stats. You choose which stat with statTarget. See table below!
⏱ 3 turns then fades

🎯 Which stat does statup / statdwn change?

When you create a statup or statdwn move, you must add a statTarget field to tell the game which stat to boost or lower. Each type only changes one specific stat!

statTarget value Which stat changes 📈 statup effect 📉 statdwn effect
"atk" or "power" ⚔️ Attack power +20 ATK for 3 turns −20 ATK for 3 turns
"speed" 💨 Speed +20 SPD for 3 turns −20 SPD for 3 turns
"defense" 🛡️ Defense +15 DEF for 3 turns −15 DEF for 3 turns
"accuracy" 🎯 Accuracy +15 ACC for 3 turns −15 ACC for 3 turns
⚠️ Important: Stacking the same status doesn't help! Having five sleep moves only counts as one. Variety is way more powerful than repetition!
5

Designing Your Moves

Your Fakemon can have up to 8 moves. Each move is an object inside the "moves" list of your JSON file.

FieldRequired?What to put here
id✅ YesA short nickname with no spaces — like fire_bite. Every move must have a different id.
name✅ YesThe display name players see in battle, including an emoji!
desc💡 Rec.A one-sentence description of what the move does.
speed✅ Yesfast goes before everything · medium is normal · slow goes last (but hits harder)
damage✅ YesAn array [min, max]. E.g. [15, 25] = 15 to 25 damage. Use [0, 0] for no damage. Use negative numbers like [-25, -25] to heal yourself!
status❌ NoOne status to apply: "poison", "burn", "paralyze", "sleep", "confuse", "flinch", "statup", "statdwn". Use null for none.
statusChance❌ NoHow likely the status is to apply. 1.0 = always. 0.5 = 50% chance. 0.35 = 35% chance. Use 0 if no status.
statTarget⚠️ If statup/dwWhich stat to boost or lower: "atk", "speed", "defense", or "accuracy". Required when status is statup or statdwn.
statuses 🆕 Advanced Apply multiple statuses at once! Instead of the status field, use an array. See examples below.
💡 Strategy tip: A Fakemon with ALL damage moves has no tricks. A Fakemon with ALL status moves but no damage might never finish the fight. Mix it up — try 3–4 damage moves, 1–2 status moves, 1 stat move, and 1 heal move!

Strategy Tips

The power system is smart — it actually simulates battles to score your Fakemon. Here's how to make a strong one:

✅ DO

  • Mix damage, status, and stat moves
  • Include at least one heal move
  • Use statup to build power early
  • Use statdwn to cripple fast enemies
  • Use a statTarget that matches your playstyle

❌ DON'T

  • Use 5 of the same move type
  • Forget a damage move entirely
  • Put all your power in super low HP
  • Skip the statTarget on a statup/statdwn move
  • Use accuracy: 100 if you don't mean it
💡 A clever design with good synergy between moves will beat a boring high-damage machine most of the time.

🐛 Bug Testing Your Fakemon

Something's not working? Don't panic — that's totally normal. This section will walk you through exactly how to find and fix problems with your Fakemon file.

📋 The Basic Testing Workflow

Every time you make a change, follow these steps:

1

Make your change in the editor

Edit your .json file in the github.dev editor (the browser-based editor that opens when you press . on your repo, or navigate to github.dev/your-repo-url). Fix one thing at a time so you know what worked.

2

Commit & push to GitHub

Your changes do NOT go live until you commit them. In GitHub Dev, click the Source Control panel (the branching icon on the left), write a short message like fix missing comma, then click Commit & Push. If you skip this, the game will still show your old file!

3

Wait for GitHub Pages to rebuild — up to 2 minutes!

After you push, GitHub has to rebuild the site. This takes anywhere from 30 seconds to 2 minutes. There is no sound or notification — you just have to wait. If you reload immediately, you'll see your old file.

🌿 Growing while you wait… 2:00
Waiting for GitHub Pages to rebuild…
4

Hard refresh the page — don't just press reload!

Your browser keeps a copy of old files in its cache. A normal refresh just shows you that cached (old) copy. You need a hard refresh to force the browser to download the new files from GitHub. On a Chromebook:

Ctrl + Shift + R or Ctrl + F5 or Hold Ctrl and click the reload button 🔄

⚠️ If you're not hard refreshing, you might be testing OLD code and wasting your time!

5

Check the debug panel

Open the game page. The 🐛 DEBUG panel at the top of the screen shows you exactly what happened when your file loaded. Red = broken. Yellow = warning. Green = good.

🚨 Common Errors & How to Fix Them

💥
Broken JSON — won't load at all
Usually a missing comma, quote, or bracket
What you see in the debug log
🐛 DEBUG filter: ✗ ERROR
[00:01:04]✗FAILED to load myfakemon.json: Invalid JSON: Unexpected token } in JSON at position 312
[00:01:04]✗File should end with } (closing curly brace)
How to fix it

Your JSON has a typo somewhere. The most common causes:

  • Missing comma between two moves — every move except the last one needs a , after its closing }
  • Extra comma after the last move in the list — the last item before ] must NOT have a comma
  • Missing quote around a value — all text values need "quotes"
  • Using single quotes like 'fire' — JSON only accepts "double quotes"
  • Mismatched brackets — every { needs a matching } and every [ needs a ]
💡 Paste your JSON into jsonlint.com — it will point to the exact line with the problem!
🔍
File not found (404 error)
The game can't find your JSON file
What you see in the debug log
🐛 DEBUG filter: ✗ ERROR
[00:01:02]✗FAILED to load fakemon/Blazkit.json: HTTP 404 — file not found
How to fix it
  • Make sure your file is inside the fakemon/ folder, not the root of the project
  • Check the filename in fakemonlist.json matches your file exactly — including capitals and the .json extension
  • Capitalization matters! Blazkit.json ≠ blazkit.json. GitHub is case-sensitive.
  • Check that you actually committed and pushed the file — if you only saved it locally in GitHub Dev without committing, it won't exist on the server
❓
Unknown status or bad statTarget
A move has a typo in its status or statTarget value
What you see in the debug log
🐛 DEBUG filter: ✗ ERROR
[00:01:03]✗Move 3 (🔥 Fire Snap): unknown status "Burn" — did you mean "burn"?
[00:01:03]✗Move 5 (📈 Power Up): "statTarget" must be one of: atk, speed, defense, accuracy
How to fix it

Status names and statTarget values are case-sensitive — all lowercase, no capitals:

poison burn paralyze sleep confuse flinch statup statdwn

Valid statTarget values: atk · speed · defense · accuracy

🎯
statup / statdwn move does nothing
Forgot to add statTarget — it defaults to "atk" silently
What you see in the debug log (⚠ WARN tab)
🐛 DEBUG filter: ⚠ WARN
[00:01:03]⚠Move 4 (🛡️ Iron Shell): has status statup but no statTarget — defaulting to "atk"
How to fix it

Add "statTarget": "defense" (or whichever stat you meant to boost) to the move object. See the statTarget table in Section 4 for all options.

⚔️
Fakemon loads but battles never end
No damage moves — it can never knock out the enemy
What you see in the debug log (⚠ WARN tab)
🐛 DEBUG filter: ⚠ WARN
[00:01:02]⚠No damage moves found — Fakemon may never finish a fight
How to fix it

Add at least one move where "damage" has two positive numbers, like [15, 22]. A move with [0, 0] deals no damage.

🏷️
Unknown type error
The type field has a typo or wrong capitalisation
What you see in the debug log
🐛 DEBUG filter: ✗ ERROR
[00:01:02]✗Unknown type "fire" — valid types: Fire, Water, Air, Earth, Electric, Ice, Poison, Dark, Ghost, Metal, Nature, Normal
How to fix it

Type names start with a capital letter: "Fire" not "fire", "Electric" not "electric".

📋
My Fakemon doesn't appear on the select screen
File exists but wasn't added to fakemonlist.json
What you see in the debug log (ℹ INFO tab)
🐛 DEBUG filter: ✓ OK
[00:01:01]✓fakemonlist.json OK — 3 entries found
[00:01:02]✓Loaded 3 / 3 Fakemon successfully

If the count doesn't include yours, your file isn't in the list.

How to fix it

Open fakemon/fakemonlist.json and add your filename to the array. Make sure the filename matches exactly — including .json at the end and correct capitalisation.

🗄️
I fixed the bug but it's still broken!
The browser is showing you an old cached version
What's happening

Your browser saves copies of files so pages load faster. This is called a cache. When you update your file and reload normally, the browser often shows the old cached copy — not the new one you just uploaded. This makes it look like your fix didn't work even when it did!

How to hard refresh on a Chromebook
Ctrl + Shift + R Hard refresh (most reliable)
Ctrl + F5 Same as above, alternate shortcut
Hold Ctrl and click the 🔄 reload button in the address bar
⚠️ A normal reload (Ctrl + R or the 🔄 button alone) is NOT enough. Always use Ctrl+Shift+R after pushing changes!

🔀 Opening Your Pull Request

A pull request (PR) is how you officially submit your Fakemon to the shared class game. It's a request to merge your branch into the main game so everyone can fight against your creature.

⚠️ Do NOT open a pull request until your Fakemon works. If you submit broken code, it can break the game for the whole class — not just you. Test first. PR second. Always.

Why test before you PR?

When your pull request gets merged, your code goes into the shared main branch that everyone's game runs from. Think of it like this:

✅ What a good PR looks like

  • Fakemon loads with no red errors in the debug panel
  • You can select it on the battle screen
  • You can actually finish a battle with it
  • Your checklist in Section 11 is fully checked off
  • The game still works after you added your Fakemon

❌ What breaks things for everyone

  • A JSON syntax error that stops the whole file list from loading
  • A filename in fakemonlist.json that doesn't exist
  • A Fakemon that crashes the battle engine mid-fight
  • Submitting and saying "I'll fix it later" — later never comes

✅ The "Ready to PR" checklist

Before you open the PR, you should be able to say yes to all of these:

🟢 I opened the game page and my Fakemon appeared on the selection screen
🟢 The debug panel showed zero red (✗ ERROR) messages when the page loaded
🟢 I selected my Fakemon and started a battle — it actually ran
🟢 The battle ended properly (win or lose) — it didn't freeze

How to open the pull request

1

Make sure all your changes are committed and pushed

In the github.dev editor, check the Source Control panel (the branch icon on the left sidebar). It should say 0 changes — nothing pending. If there are changes listed, commit and push them first.

2

Go to your repository on github.com

Navigate to github.com/your-username/your-repo. GitHub will often show a yellow banner saying "Your branch is ahead of main — Compare & pull request." If you see it, click that button.

3

If no banner, click "Pull requests" → "New pull request"

Click the Pull requests tab at the top of your repo, then the green New pull request button. Make sure the base branch is main and the compare branch is yours.

4

Write a title and description

Give your PR a clear title like "Add Blazkit — Fire type Fakemon". In the description, briefly describe your Fakemon: its type, its strategy, and anything interesting about its moves. This helps your teacher review it.

5

Click "Create pull request" — you're done!

Your teacher will review it. They might leave comments asking you to fix something before it gets merged. Check back on the PR page to see if there's any feedback.

💡 Can I update my PR after submitting? Yes! If your teacher asks for a fix, just make the change in the github.dev editor, commit and push, and the PR will automatically update. You don't need to close and reopen it.
🚀 Once your PR is merged, your Fakemon is officially part of the game. Everyone in the class can battle against it. That's your code, live, in a real shared project. That's real software development!
